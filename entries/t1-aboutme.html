Tabletop for the Mind

There are hundreds of great tabletop games out there. Some more complex than others, but all fun with the right people. Some of my current favorites are Arkham Horror, Coup, Catan, and Skull King. However, none of these will ever match the complexity and level of entertainment that Dungeons and Dragons aka D&D can provide. Without a doubt THE nerdiest of games. But, only weirdos and socially awkward people play that, right?

Wrong! D&D is so much more than people playing make-believe in a fantasy world, killing dragons and exploring dungeons. Although there IS a lot of that, it's in the title after all. D&D is an experience, a way for people to spend some time together to create a collective narrative; one in which the players are the characters, heroes and sometimes even villains of that story. It is a theater play at home. This can range anywhere from a Sherlock Homes murder mystery to an over the top battle of kingdoms.

I've been playing with my friends for a little over a year now. And let me tell you, it is so much fun. Gather up, face some obstacles, use your wit, make some choices, throw your dice, and let fate decide the outcome.
